Spaghetti Bolognese

35–40 mins Serves 4 Comfort

Ingredients

  • 400g dried spaghetti
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 500g ground beef
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 400g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water or stock
  • Salt & pepper
  • Parmesan, to serve
  • Fresh basil (optional)

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté onion for 3–4 minutes until soft. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.
  2. Add beef, season with salt and pepper, and cook until browned, breaking up with a spoon.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, then add crushed tomatoes, water/stock, oregano, and basil. Simmer gently 15–20 minutes. Adjust seasoning; add sugar if too acidic.
  4. Meanwhile, cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve a splash of pasta water.
  5. Toss spaghetti with sauce, loosening with pasta water if needed. Serve with grated Parmesan and basil.

Tips

  • For deeper flavor, simmer 30+ minutes.
  • Use half beef, half pork for a richer sauce.
  • Leftovers keep 3 days in the fridge.

Nutrition (approx.)

650 kcal • 28g protein • 75g carbs • 24g fat
